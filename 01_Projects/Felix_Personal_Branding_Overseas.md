## üí° Core Beliefs & Contrarian Views

### Belief #1: AI Won't Replace You‚ÄîYour Inability to Architect Will
**Mainstream View**: "AI will replace all jobs"
**My View**: AI is a **leverage tool**, not a replacement. People who can't think in systems will be marginalized. People who can architect (design workflows, set standards, judge outputs) become supermen.

**Why I Believe This**:
- Moore's Law is hitting limits; AGI isn't coming soon
- Companies still hire aggressively (Parkinson's Law: headcount = ambition, not just productivity)
- AI expands what's possible, which expands how many people you need

### Belief #2: Ship Ugly, Ship Fast, Ship Often
**Mainstream View**: "Perfect your product before launch"
**My View**: Get to market at 60%, then iterate with real users. Waiting for 100% means you're already obsolete.

**The Caveat**: This works for **yourself**, not always for **B2B sales**. Customers want certainty, not co-creation. Be agile internally; give clients finished solutions.

### Belief #3: AI + Human Hybrid > Pure AI or Pure Human
**The Formula**:
- AI handles 80% of grunt work (data gathering, pattern matching, draft generation)
- Humans handle 20% of critical thinking (framework design, edge cases, emotional nuance)

**Real Example**: My team ships market analysis reports in 1/10th the time by having AI pull data, humans design insights.

## üõ†Ô∏è Practical Guidelines for Content Creation

### Before Writing Anything, Ask:
1. **Is this contrarian?** (If everyone agrees, it's boring)
2. **Do I have real examples?** (No theory without proof)
3. **Would AI write this?** (If yes, rewrite with more personality)
4. **Does this help builders?** (No clickbait, only substance)

### The Felix Content Checklist
- [ ] Specific numbers included ($X ‚Üí $Y, N%)
- [ ] Company names mentioned (Anker, Nike, etc.)
- [ ] Personal experience shared ("I built...", "I failed...")
- [ ] Counterintuitive angle stated upfront
- [ ] 3 concrete examples in body
- [ ] Bold closing (claim, action, or open question)
- [ ] No AI-washing language ("game-changer", "revolutionary")

### Topics to Avoid (Unless Contrarian Angle Exists)
- Generic "AI trends 2026" (everyone covers this)
- "Top 10 AI tools" (listicle hell)
- Surface-level news aggregation (add original analysis or skip)
- Anything I haven't personally tested/experienced
